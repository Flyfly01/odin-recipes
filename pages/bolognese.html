Bolognese

A thick rich hearty bolognese with a great depth of flavor! Leave it on the stopetop to simmer for a couple hours, return later on, and a pot of the best ragu you've ever made will be awaiting you.

Bolognese is a meat based sauce in Italian cuisine. It is typically paired with some form of pasta to make dishes such as lasagne or spaghetti bolognese - the latter of which we'll be focusing on today. This recipe uses a base of celery, carrots and onions along with other simple ingredients to create a harmonious mix of flavors and richness.

WHAT YOU NEED TO MAKE THIS RECIPE

Ingredients

  • 100g celery
  • 100g carrot
  • 100g onion
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • 2 tablespoons chopped parsley
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 14.5oz cans tomatoes - chopped
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 113ml red wine

Steps

  1. Chop all vegetables and combine in 1 bowl. Put garlic in separate bowl
  2. Heat oil in pan and add garlic and cook for 1 minute on medium heat
  3. Add in vegetables and salt and cook for 5 minutes until soft
  4. Add beef and cook until brown ~10 minutes
  5. While beef is cooking, chop tomatoes in food processor. Also chop parsley
  6. Once beef is cooked, add wine and cook for 5 minutes
  7. Add tomatoes and parsley
  8. Turn up heat to bring to a boil
  9. Once boiling, cover and reduce heat to simmer for 2 hours
